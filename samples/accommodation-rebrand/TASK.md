# TASK.md

Build a standalone sample website for this folder.

## Sample Identity
- Title: Accommodation Rebrand Website
- Slug: `accommodation-rebrand`
- Type: Multi-page hospitality rebrand rollout site

## Objective
- Create a hospitality website that expresses a refreshed brand identity while preserving trust and usability.
- The page should feel like a successful digital relaunch for an accommodation business.
- The final website copy should be written in Japanese for prospective Japanese clients and guests.

## Inputs
- Read `../../AGENTS.md`
- Read `../../SAMPLE_SPEC.md`
- Read `../../CODING_RULES.md`
- Read `../../REVIEW_CHECKLIST.md`
- Read `./COLOR_SCHEMA.md`

## Output
- Create a 4-page static website
- Required pages:
  - `index.html` for the renewed brand overview and booking entry
  - `rooms.html` for stay highlights and room positioning
  - `facilities.html` for dining, amenities, and upgraded services
  - `access.html` for access, contact, FAQ, and inquiry guidance
- Keep shared assets inside the sample folder
- Create `css/styles.css`
- Create `js/scripts.js` only if interaction adds clear value

## Audience
- Existing and new Japanese guests comparing accommodation options
- Users evaluating whether the property feels modern and trustworthy
- Mobile visitors seeking quick understanding of the updated brand

## Design Direction
- Mood: refreshed, polished, contemporary
- Communicate brand renewal through cleaner layouts and stronger hierarchy
- Balance emotional branding with practical booking cues

## Content Direction
- Use realistic Japanese copy
- Avoid placeholder text
- Present the accommodation as newly refined but dependable
- Keep the tone welcoming and brand-conscious

## Required Sections
- Hero:
  Japanese rebrand-style headline, short promise, booking CTA
- New Brand Story:
  What changed and what experience is being emphasized
- Rooms and Stay Appeal:
  Featured accommodation highlights
- Facilities and Services:
  Dining, amenities, or upgraded guest experience points
- Brand Features:
  Design, comfort, locality, or hospitality differentiators
- Guest Trust:
  Reviews, reassurance points, or continuity with past trust
- Final CTA:
  Booking or inquiry section
- Footer:
  Access, contact-style details, and navigation

## Interaction Guidance
- Refined reveal or hover effects are acceptable
- Respect `prefers-reduced-motion`
- The page should feel modern, not animated for its own sake

## Visual Constraints
- Follow `COLOR_SCHEMA.md`
- Use accent color to mark the updated brand identity and CTA elements
- Keep the overall tone polished and contemporary

## Success Criteria
- The page looks portfolio-ready
- It reads as a believable accommodation rebrand website
- It works on mobile and desktop
- It can be reviewed using `../../REVIEW_CHECKLIST.md` without obvious gaps
- The Japanese copy feels appropriate for a real hospitality rebrand
